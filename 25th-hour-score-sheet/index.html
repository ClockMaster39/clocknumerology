Rules

Categories are comprised of the subject-at-hand. In the case of a cipher that lists "Christianity" as its metaphysics, the categories for Jesus might include "King and God" & "Messiah".

To make the fairest rules possible the most universal constant (from numbers 0 - 100) is 1 according to Benford's Law. From 0-100, the number 1 occurs more often as far as universal constants are concerned. Therefore I concluded that a contestant scoring at least 1 point in every category is most fair (hence the "Basic Win" award).

NOTE: brainstorming whether how many points must be scored in every category became more complex and unstable when requiring anything more than one point. For instance, a) an author of a cipher has had a great sense of accomplishment in scoring at least one point in every category (thereby making it accurate -at least technically); and, b) debating which number represents the source (ie. god) becomes merely a matter of personal opinion and belief. Thanks to Benford's Law the scoring not only settled every dispute but simplified the entire, scoring method. And this scoring makes sense: a house is not 100% unless it has at least one piece of furniture, an appliance, a garage, electricity, etc. etc.

A score can be counted by means of "word for word" or "measurement". For example, "God = 35" while "Thirty-Five = 355" & "God = 35" while "3/5 hands equal 3:25" because "The Number of God = 325".

Each Cipher must result in at least one point in every category to qualitfy with a max, bonus score of 1 point. If a cipher scores more than 1 point in at least one category then all other categories must get at least that same score for it to count. For example, if said cipher scores 2 points in one category, while scoring 1 point in all remaining categories -every 2nd point does not count. Any extra points count as a bonus point of (+1) onto grand total.

If a contestant scores at least 1 point in every category -it counts as a "Basic Win" (with just an additional one bonus point).

BONUS POINTS: A bonus point is where a cipher gives a result that "answers back". For example, "Jesus = 87" while "Eighty-Seven = 208" where "The Number of Jesus = 208". This results in a bonus point worth (+1) onto grand total.

Bonus points may not exceed the number of points in every category. For example, if said cipher scores 3 points in every category, no more than 3 bonus points (+3) can be added to the grand total in the original scoring-round. If said cipher scores 5 points in one category and only 3 points in every other category, the max would still just be (+3) bonus points. Max bonus-points available are equal to the total number of categories (eg. 4 categories = 4 max bonus points in original scoring-round) -where each Bonus Round won will add +2 to contestant's grand total (must not exceed the orginal win that is awarded; meaning, a contestant winning the "Gold Win" award can not win a "Platinum Win" by winning more bonus points).

A contestant who scores, say, 32 points can attempt to achieve a "Gold Win" (High Tier/39 points) by entering as many bonus points as possible. A "Gold Win" is getting a max of 4 points in every category. In conclusion, that contestant will need to win 3 or 4 bonus rounds to achieve a high tier "Gold Win" where +2 points is added to that contestant's grand total for every 4 additional bonus points. Earning the first 4 points in the Bonus Category (of "Gold Win") will unlock the "Bonus Round" so that contestant may try to earn the high tier win that was awarded.

The "Perfect Win" award is the only achievement that does not require any bonus points to unlock it.
