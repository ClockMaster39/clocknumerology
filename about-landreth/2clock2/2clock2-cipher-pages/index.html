How Was The "2clock2" Cipher Made?

For letter 'A' gets a value of '1' because 'one' is the universal starting point. What is 1 p.m.? Correct! From this we can give letter 'B' a value of '13'. For letter 'C' we study the 1st hour-marker. 'One' is '1' -it is also the 5 second marker. So, 1 + 5 = 6; moreover, 12 'divides' the day. From this we have 6 / 12 = 0.5. Finally, letter 'C' gets a value of '5'. For letter 'D' we study the 2nd hour-marker. 'Two' is '2'; moreover, 'Two' is also the 10 second marker. So, 2 + 10 = 12. Moreover 12 'divides' the day. From this we get 12 / 12 = 1. Finally, letter 'D' gets a value of '1'. So on and so forth through letter 'M'.
